Rick's leadership and influence within the group has now overgrown and fully overpowers the authority that Shane once had. Rick struggles with this responsibility however, attempting to weigh being fair and doing what he believes to be right, no matter what the rules of society were like in the past. In the second season premiere, "What Lies Ahead", Carl is accidentally shot during the group's search for Sophia (Madison Lintz), Carol's (Melissa McBride) daughter, who is chased away by walkers during the group's escape from Atlanta. In the episode "Bloodletting", the shooter, Otis takes them to the isolated farm occupied by Hershel (Scott Wilson), a veterinarian who works to save Carl's life by using a transfusion of Rick's blood. Carl then recovers with Shane's help of medical supplies in the episode "Cherokee Rose". In the episode "Secrets", Lori tells Rick that she is pregnant and reveals to Rick that she and Shane had been together after she believed he died. In the mid-season finale, "Pretty Much Dead Already", the group discovers that Hershel has kept walkers in the barn, including his family, believing them to be ill. Despite Rick's refusal, Shane opens the barn and kills the walkers inside, until one is revealed to be Sophia. Rick shoots a reanimated Sophia, as the group mourns in devastation. In the mid-season premiere, "Nebraska", Rick is forced to kill two men in cold blood in a bar to protect the group but takes one of their group members, Randall (Michael Zegen), as a prisoner in the episode "Triggerfinger". Shane, however, believes he should be killed. In the episode "Better Angels", Rick decides to keep the prisoner alive. Tensions between Rick and Shane have heated up to the point where Shane attempts to assassinate Rick by leading him out to the woods during a search for Randall after Shane kills him. Rick talks him out of it but stabs Shane in the heart in the last second. Shane reanimates only to be shot by Carl. In the season finale, "Beside the Dying Fire", the shot attracts a walker horde who overrun the farm. Rick reveals to the escaped group that they are all infected, learning of this from Dr. Jenner at the CDC but only now believing it. He tells Lori he killed Shane to protect himself as well as the group, but his wife is horrified that she divided the men. Rick's cold demeanor shocks the group as he dares any would-be traitors to abandon the safety of the group.
